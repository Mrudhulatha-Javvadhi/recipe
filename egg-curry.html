Egg Curry

Ingredients:

  • 4 hard-boiled eggs, peeled and halved
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 green chili, slit lengthwise (optional, adjust to tast)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • Salt to taste
  • Chopped fresh cilantro for garnish

Steps:

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and fry until they start to sizzle.
  2. Add the finely chopped onion to the skillet and sauté until golden brown, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Add the minced garlic, grated ginger, and slit green chili (if using) to the skillet. Sauté for another 2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Stir in the ground coriander, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and garam masala. Cook for 1-2 minutes until the spices are aromatic.
  5. Add the finely chopped tomatoes to the skillet and cook until they soften and break down, about 5 minutes.
  6. Pour in the coconut milk and stir to combine. Bring the mixture to a simmer.
  7. Gently add the halved hard-boiled eggs to the skillet, cut side up, and spoon some of the sauce over them.
  8. Cover the skillet and let the eggs simmer in the sauce for 5-7 minutes to absorb the flavors.
  9. Season the egg curry with salt to taste.
  10. Garnish the egg curry with chopped fresh cilantro before serving.
  11. Serve hot with steamed rice or naan bread.
